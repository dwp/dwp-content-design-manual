Broadcast a content crit in Slack

Tools and advice on how to get your work reviewed and how to review other people's work.

Preparing for a broadcast

A day or two before the call:

  • tell colleagues in the #content-design-crits channel that the broadcast will be taking place
  • give people a bit of detail about what the crit will be covering
  • set a reminder in the #content-design-crits channel using the format:

    • /remind #content-design-crits Content crit on subject on 27 February at 14:10

Before you start

Give yourself enough time to set up

Allow 10 minutes to:

  • make sure IT equipment and audio is working properly – it can be helpful to make a test call to a colleague to make sure everything is working
  • set up a call in Slack and allow people to join
  • share your screen in Slack

Agree who will monitor online questions

Someone will need to pass the questions and comments typed in Slack to the presenter during the broadcast.

It helps if this is someone other than the content designer who is talking about their content and responding to verbal questions.

Running the broadcast

Start a call in the #content-design-crits channel:

  • Select the phone icon at the top right
  • Confirm you'd like to start a call. Give the call a name if you'd like, then click Start new call
  • Any member of the channel can join by selecting Join this call

More guidance on making calls in Slack

Share your screen

During the call, select the Share your screen icon so that participants can see the content you want them to review.

More guidance on screen sharing

Introduce your content

Welcome people on the call and do a short introduction to restate what the crit will cover.

Other hints and tips

During the call:

  • avoid sitting in a noisy area – background noise can be disruptive
  • if you’re in the same room as someone else who’s on the same Slack call, use only one microphone to avoid audio feedback
  • stay on mute unless you need to talk – remember your micprophone will be live by default when you join
  • it’s okay not to say anything – we’re there to learn as well as help
